# LERMS Game Loop Attractor

## Ontology Source

[../ontology.md](../ontology.md) is the durable source for world meaning. This attractor should stay mechanically useful, but it must not outrank the ontology.

## Core Loop

The glove guards a hoard of goins at the crown of the hill-of-hills.

Lerms climb toward the hoard. When a lerm reaches the crown, it steals a goin and runs away. If the caster hand beams that lerm, it drops the goin. The goin rolls down the terrain, and nearby lerms reroute to chase it. The player is not merely killing enemies; they are managing a disgusting little economy of desire.

Longer-term, stolen wealth should not only leave the score pile. Lerms metabolize it into structures, growths, beacons, gates, bunkers, and little civic disasters. That makes theft a terrain/ecology mutation, not just a decrement.

## Why This Works

The hand-tracking substrate is strongest at:

- fingertip screen position,
- finger direction,
- finger identity,
- rough pose/fist state,
- expressive local finger motion.

It is weaker at absolute hand depth. The game should therefore make finger direction and posture load-bearing while making absolute hand depth optional or decorative.

The hill-of-hills solves the old depth problem by giving the world its own depth. The hand does not need to move through depth; it casts into a world that has depth.

## Lerm Species

Red lerms are pop idiots: short, eager, momentum-forward, easy to panic, satisfying to blast.

Blue lerms are substrate believers: longer, lower, topology-loving, harder to blast directly, vulnerable to pooling and rerouting, and dangerously good at turning stolen wealth into architecture.

Yellow lerms are insulting little opportunists: skittish, fake-out prone, weirdly responsive to state-changing fluids, false shinies, reflections, dropped buttons, and rumor fields.

Species must read as silhouette, posture, motion, and fluid constitution, not merely stat variants.

## Finger Juice

Index juice is sharp blue knockback. It hits red lerms and sends them tumbling into their own convictions.

Middle juice is white adhesive gunk. It gums terrain, slows lerms, catches goins, and may accidentally provide blue lerms with construction material.

Ring juice is green fertilizer. It grows moss, vines, fungus pads, obstruction flora, and regrettably may feed lerms.

Thumb juice is amber slick. It is heavy, warm, goin-moving, and gutter-making.

Pinky juice is purple weirdness. Yellow lerms love it, which is the first sign of danger.

The player should feel like the hand is a multivalent gland organ, not a generic cannon.

## Terrain

The terrain is a cylinder-channel hill-of-hills:

- broad low center,
- rounded hills and basins,
- macro channel curvature,
- slow roiling variation,
- floor and ceiling bounds per terrain field,
- period controls for how the terrain moves over time.

The terrain should eventually affect fluid and lerm routing, not just background presentation.

## Frame

Underhill is not tutorial flavor. It is the interpretive frame: the player studies the hill from below before learning to act above. The repo should preserve that sense of damp civic substrate, lerm terrarium, trades, glove law, and suspicious machinery when it chooses menus, receipts, loading screens, debug rooms, or onboarding.
